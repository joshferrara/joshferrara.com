> 💡 I use a lot of apps to make my life easier. You probably know a lot of the apps on this list, but there may be a few that you haven’t heard of that could add some value to your life!

### ⭐ My Favorite Apps

If you only check out a few apps on this list, these are my top recommendations.

- [1Password](https://1password.com/)
  - You should be using a password manager. The one built into your phone or computer is okay, but using a dedicated app is a huge way you can bump up your security. I’ve been using 1Password for over a decade, and I can tell you they are the best.
- [Deliveries](https://deliveries.app/)
  - The greatest package tracking app. You’ll want this on Mac and iOS.
- [Drafts](https://getdrafts.com/)
  - This is my scratch note pad all day long. Syncs between Mac and iPhone. Has actions to send a note to other places (for example: a text, an email, a tweet, etc). To quote their marketing material: This is where all of my text starts.
- [Hand Mirror](https://handmirror.app/)
  - If you’re on video calls throughout your day, this is a quick way to see your camera before you jump into a call. Check your hair or surroundings, avoid embarrassment.
- [Home Control](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/homecontrol-menu-for-homekit/id1547121417?mt=12)
  - If you use Apple HomeKit to control any smart devices in your home, this little app lets you trigger smart devices from your Mac, with keyboard shortcuts.
  - Pro tip: Pair this with a StreamDeck to have a physical control center for devices all around your house.
- [Hazel](https://www.noodlesoft.com/)
  - Automate file organization tasks on your Mac. Move screenshots to the trash after 5 minutes. Delete downloads older than 30 days. Organize photos from your camera into sorted folders by date. This little workflow tool is extremely powerful.
- [Loom](https://www.loom.com/)
  - We live in an asynchronous world, but not all communication is best through text. I use this tool to send quick video messages, and even screen share to clearly explain an idea as quickly as possible.
- [Notion](https://www.notion.so/)
  - I subscribe to the idea that our brains are best used to solve problems, not remembering data. This app is truly an extension of my brain, it’s where I store so much data. Family details, household projects, hobbies, side work, travel plans... Truly all parts of my life are stored here. If you’ve ever wanted a single place to keep up with your life, this can be that place. If you can’t tell, I’m passionate about this app.
  - Pro tip: Check out my guide for a deeper dive.
- [Paprika](https://www.paprikaapp.com/)
  - Keep up with your recipes, build grocery lists, scale to serve more guests. If you cook, this app is a game-changer. Get it on iOS too and sync between platforms!
- [Raycast](https://www.raycast.com/)
  - This is a small app that can boost your productivity in a ton of areas. It gives you a small text input field from which you can quickly trigger different features. Search your computer, do quick math, ask Google something, trigger an automated workflow, control your music... It does a lot, and I use it constantly.
- [Rocket](https://matthewpalmer.net/rocket/)
  - Slack-style emoji picker for every text field on the Mac. This should be built into the operating system, but since it is not, you need this free app.
- [Soulver](https://soulver.app/)
  - One part notepad, one part calculator. If you find yourself pulling out a spreadsheet to do quick calculations, this app is a game-changer. Use natural language to process through calculations and make your life easier. From splitting bills at the restaurant, to calculating time across a project, if it involves numbers, you want Soulver.

### 🗒️ General Purpose Apps

This is a long list, I get it. But I think these are all useful and worth checking out!

- [Backblaze](https://www.backblaze.com/)
  - You should have an automated off-site backup system on your Mac (or PC!). If you don’t have one already, you should use Backblaze. It’s $7/month and backs up everything on your computer. Computer broken or stolen? They’ll mail you a drive with your files. Seriously, if you don’t already have a backup system in place (and even if you do) you should be using Backblaze.
- [Bartender](https://www.macbartender.com/)
  - If you’ve got a lot of apps in your Mac menu bar and the top of your screen is getting cluttered, this app helps you organize (and optionally hide) those apps. Simple quality of life improvement.
- [Caffeine](https://intelliscapesolutions.com/apps/caffeine)
  - A one-click app that disables your Mac’s auto-sleep settings. Useful if you want to make sure a download completes or your computer stays awake to finish a task.
- [Calcbot](https://tapbots.com/calcbot/mac/)
  - I wish this was the calculator that shipped with the Mac. A simple calculator, with a running history of your calculations to quickly jump back to, and a unit conversion tool. I’ve been using this one for more than a decade and it’s still one of my favs.
- [Choosy](https://www.choosyosx.com/)
  - If you use multiple web browsers, this app lets you specific which links open in which browser. Think “Chrome for my work sites, Safari for personal”. Set up rules to auto-route your links, or have Choosy ask you when you click a new link.
  - Pro tip: You can also use this to route things like Spotify, Figma, or Notion links to open in their app instead of in a browser.
- [CleanMyMac X](https://macpaw.com/cleanmymac)
  - Clean up junk on your Mac to keep things running smoothly. No matter how tidy you think you are, your computer needs a deep clean from time to time. This is the app.
- [CloudApp](https://www.getcloudapp.com/)
  - I use this one to auto-upload screenshots I take to a web view that I can share a link for. Easily share screenshots for work or personal, and give people a space to give quick feedback on a simple page. The app does much more than just screenshots, but this is the feature that made me fall in love years ago.
- [Day One](https://dayoneapp.com/)
  - If you’ve ever wanted to be better about journaling or documenting your life, this one can probably help you out. On Mac or iOS, the app is easy to use, and adds helpful data about your day like location, weather, and even suggests photos you took or social posts from that specific day.
  - Pro tip: You can order a physical book of all or a selection of your journal entries. Have a special season of life that you want to remember in physical form? Do this.
- [Divvy](https://mizage.com/windivvy/)
  - Quickly organize the windows on your Mac into neat, orderly grids. You can use keyboard shortcuts or the onscreen grid to quickly snap your windows into whatever configuration works best for you. This is another one I can’t live without.
- [Ecamm Live](https://www.ecamm.com/)
  - If you find yourself wanting to stream or capture video from your computer, this is a powerful option. Streaming to Twitch, YouTube, Facebook etc, are all easy with this. You’ll have the option to switch between cameras, screen shares and other content.
- [Mmhmm](https://www.mmhmm.app/)
  - If you give presentations over video calls, this app lets you incorporate on-screen slides and images in some really fun and engaging ways. A nice upgrade from screen-sharing a deck.
- [Figma](https://www.figma.com/)
  - The future of design tools, built as a web app for live collaboration and sharing. I fell in love with this tool at work, and now I use it for nearly all design work.
- [HandBrake](https://handbrake.fr/)
  - If you need to grab or convert video files from one format to another, this is the tool you want.
- [iA Writer](https://ia.net/writer)
  - My preferred writing app when I need to zone in and focus. With a nearly blank screen, everything fades away leaving nothing but your text on screen. Very refreshing.
- [iStat Menus](https://bjango.com/mac/istatmenus/)
  - A helpful menu bar app that gives you insights into your computer health. Battery, power consumption, memory usage, available storage, network traffic, and more. Very helpful.
- [Little Snitch](https://www.obdev.at/products/littlesnitch/index.html)
  - If you’ve ever been curious to know when apps on your computer make a connection to the internet, this will give you an alert when that happens. Also allows you to create rules about which apps can connect to the internet, and apply those rules based on which wifi network you’re currently connected to. Very helpful if you want to save data while tethering or prevent data tracking on shared networks.
- [Micro Snitch](https://www.obdev.at/products/microsnitch/index.html)
  - If you’ve ever wondered if an app uses your microphone or camera without you knowing? This app gives you an alert any time your mic or camera are activated.
- [Loopback](https://rogueamoeba.com/loopback/)
  - This one is a powerhouse. If you ever find yourself wanting to route audio from one app on your mac into another app, or from an app to a specific speaker or output, this app will be your best friend. Add background music to your video calls. Make Spotify play to headphones but youtube play to speakers. This app puts you in control.
- [OrgChart](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/orgchart-organization-chart/id1150742969)
  - If you find yourself needing to create org charts, this is the cleanest app I’ve found for the job. Easy to organize, include headshots, title, department, role. It’s a one trick pony but it’s a very useful trick.
- [Pastebot](https://tapbots.com/pastebot/)
  - If you’ve never used a clipboard manager, you really should check this out. Every time you copy some text on your computer, the last thing that you copied is lost forever out of your clipboard. With a clipboard manager, you get a running list of the things you’ve copied, and can quickly jump back to previous items in your clipboard and past those. This is one of those things that I can’t use a computer without now.
- [Reeder](https://reederapp.com/)
  - If you know what an RSS reader is, then I’ll just tell you this is the cleanest reader for Mac. If you don’t know what an RSS reader is, I’ll just say it’s a simple way to subscribe to the latest posts and articles from most sites on the web.
- [Screens](https://edovia.com/en/screens-mac/)
  - If you’ve ever wanted to access your computer remotely, Screens is the easiest way to do it. From your phone, tablet, or another computer, you can access your computer, see the screen, move files, etc. I use this constantly when I’m away from home and Screens makes it super simple to set up.
- [Shush](https://mizage.com/shush/)
  - Shush lets you create a push-to-talk or push-to-mute button on your keyboard to control your microphone. Very useful if you’re someone who is on video calls often.
- [StopTheMadness](https://underpassapp.com/StopTheMadness/)
  - This little Safari extension solves so many little annoyances around the web. Bypass AMP links, bypass link shorteners, hide app banners, stop link trackers, force external links into new tabs. You may not notice these things happening to you around the web, but if you do, this extension solves a ton of them.
- [Superhuman](https://superhuman.com/)
  - This is my email client of choice (for Mac and iOS). Only for Google email accounts, and honestly only something you should consider if you deal with a larger amount of email. It has a monthly subscription fee, but if you deal with a lot of email, it’s so worth it.
- [Tweetbot](https://tapbots.com/tweetbot/mac/)
  - The best Twitter app for Mac (and iOS, FWIW). If you browse [twitter.com](http://twitter.com) a lot on your Mac, this is a far more pleasant experience, and has some great features to view multiple lists at a time. I especially love this app during live events where lots of people are talking on Twitter.
- [Tot](https://tot.rocks/)
  - A small, simple way to quickly edit text. This one is a niche tool, but I find it very useful. It’s a menu bar app that gives you 7 small text scratchpads. You can paste text, edit, remove formatting, all without opening up a full-on text editor. And as a nice bonus, it syncs with the iOS app as well.
- [Transmission](https://transmissionbt.com/)
  - My favorite bit torrent client. Lightweight and simple, supports magnet links, and allows you to set up automations as well. If you’re in this world, this is the best.
- [Ulysses](https://ulysses.app/)
  - My favorite app for long-form writing. Similar focus tools to IA Writer, with the addition of great organization of outline tools. Build chapter structure, organize the sections of a talk or presentation, or even structure future blog post roadmaps. Sync w/ their iOS apps to access your writing wherever you are.
- [Via](https://www.caniusevia.com/)
  - Via let’s me configure my custom keyboards however I want. Create macros, function layers, and more.
- [Vidl](https://omz-software.com/vidl/)
  - If you ever find yourself needing to download video or audio off the internet, this will make that task much easier. Paste a URL and the download begins. Quick and easy.
- [World Clock Pro](https://worldclock.pro/)
  - I work with teammates around the world, and this makes scheduling across time zones a breeze.

### 👨🏻‍💻 Development Apps

These are most useful in software development, but you may find use for them in other ways!

- [ImageOptim](https://imageoptim.com/mac)
  - If you find yourself needing to compress images for web or app usage, this one is a quick and easy Swiss Army knife.
- [Paw](https://paw.cloud/) / [Postman](https://www.postman.com/)
  - These two apps are your secret weapon in any development tasks that involve HTTP requests. If you’re working with APIs or just a complicated Zapier task, these let you manipulate data and solve problems.
  - Postman is probably the more powerful for team work, but Paw feels more native to the Mac. I use both, and they’re both great.
- [Sublime Text](https://www.sublimetext.com/)
  - My dev text editor of choice. It’s quick, and you can extend it with plugins to make it even more powerful. Although it’s built for code, those same tools make it so powerful for manipulating other text too.
- [Tower](https://www.git-tower.com/mac)
  - The best app for working with GitHub code repositories. If you’re not comfortable with git in the command line, or just prefer a visual interface, this makes light work of complicated tasks.
- [Transmit](https://panic.com/transmit/)
  - The very best FTP client around. If you work with FTP servers at all, you’ll appreciate some of the features this adds to your workflow.

### 🤖 Hardware Required

These accompany a hardware device, which I also recommend.

- [Sonos](https://www.sonos.com/)
  - Wireless speaker system for your home or office. Connect the speakers to your wife network, and then control what’s playing. Move speakers around your house easily.
  - Pro tip: Get a Sonos soundbar for your TV and now you’ve got a wireless surround sound system.
- [Stream Deck](https://www.elgato.com/en/stream-deck)
  - A customizable control deck for your computer. Originally used by video game streamers to control their camera shots and streams, now it’s useful for controlling anything on your computer. I use mine to mute/unmute on calls, turn on my camera, turn on the lamps around my office, and even control my music.
  - Pro tip: Pair this with HomeControl to trigger commands on your HomeKit-enabled smart home devices.

### 🏅 Honorable Mentions

These need no introduction, but they’re here for completeness.

- [Adobe Creative Suite](https://www.adobe.com/creativecloud.html)
  - The OGs. Editing photos, audio, or video, designing graphics, creating motion graphics, etc. This suite of tools is the standard in make creative fields. Lots of alternatives, but I just can’t quit Adobe fully.
- [Slack](https://slack.com/)
  - A team chat tool that lets you break up conversations by topic. Lots of apps do it, but Slack does it best.
- [Spotify](https://www.spotify.com/)
  - My music app of choice. There are many ways I wish it was better, but it is unfortunately still better than the competition.
- [Zoom](https://zoom.us/)
  - We all know what Zoom is. Here’s the thing… it’s not a great app, it has some things that could use improving. But it’s where the majority of people are, so it’s worth having on your computer.
