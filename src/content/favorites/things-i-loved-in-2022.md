> 🎊 Happy New Year!
> Inspired by internet friends, I thought it would be fun to document the things I enjoyed in 2022. This isn’t a complete list, just some things that felt worth documenting and looking back on later. Enjoy!
> — [Josh](https://joshferrara.com)

## ✨ Moments

### New Orleans w/ The Boys

Before my son, Luca, started kindergarten in August, we took the boys for a weekend trip to New Orleans. They love cities, and the hotel might have been their favorite part. Traveling with them is so fun lately.

### New York City

We went to see John Mayer at Madison Square Garden with 4 of our best friends, and made a long weekend out of it. One of the best trips I’ve been on in recent memory. A highlight of the year.

### Milo’s Birthday

Milo turned 3 this year, and he was old enough to get excited and enjoy his party. There’s a photo of him blowing out his candle that may be my single favorite photo of the year.

- The Photo

### Visiting San Francisco

After a year and half living back in Louisiana, it was so much fun to spend the week with our best friends back in the Bay Area. My people and my fav city - it was good for my soul.

### Office Work w/ Gabi

We set up a second desk in our home office for Gabi. Although we don’t work in there together every day, the days we do are always a highlight of my week.

### Crawfish Boil w/ Friends & Family

After honing my recipe, we had a big crawfish boil in the spring with a bunch of family and friends. This was one of the moments I was so happy to be living back in Baton Rouge around our family.

### Short Rib Pappardelle from Boca

Since moving home, this has become our go-to for special celebrations. I’ve been to their locations around the country, but the Baton Rouge location is a cut above.

### Prince Street Pizza

We stumbled into this spot while looking for a late night snack after a concert in NYC, and proceeded to find my new favorite pizza. I think about it often.

## ⌚ Gear

### AirPods Pro

I’ve had these earbuds for a few years, and they’re still one of my most-used pieces of tech. When I’m not working, these things help me churn through podcasts, music, and audio books, and they make household chores a little nicer.

🔗 [Apple](https://www.apple.com/shop/product/MQD83AM/A/airpods-pro)

### Rama Works U80-A

I got into [building mechanical keyboards](https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PLXw_Fir5HNW-wGdL5_P1jXN46VDvB657S) during the early days of the pandemic. Aside from being a fun hobby, it has added a daily spark of joy when sitting at my desk to work. I’ve built quite a few, but this one has had the most usage in 2022.

🔗 [Rama Works](https://ramaworks.store/products/u80-a-seq2?variant=32603681914958)

### Watches

After a few years of wearing an Apple Watch daily, I started getting back to my love of traditional watches in 2020. These things bring me an unreasonable amount of daily joy, and I have fun picking out a watch to wear each morning. I found myself gravitating toward a few favorites in 2022, including my Navitimer, Casioak, and [John Mayer G-Shock](https://community.hodinkee.com/watches/153330).

🔗 [Hodinkee](https://community.hodinkee.com/members/joshferrara)

### MagSafe Charging

While not new for 2022, I finally went all in and set up MagSafe iPhone chargers at my bedside, desk, kitchen counter, in the car, and in my travel bag. It’s such a minor thing, but I love the convenience of dealing with fewer charging cables during the day.

🔗 [Apple](https://www.apple.com/shop/product/MHXH3AM/A/magsafe-charger)

### Thermapen

I’ve been cooking more over the past few years, and this instant read thermometer has become a favorite tool in the kitchen. Great cooks probably don’t need it, but it helps me stay much more precise in tracking my progress. There’s a newer model, but I like mine just fine.

🔗 [ThermoWorks](https://www.thermoworks.com/classic-thermapen/)

### Playdate

Announced in 2019, preordered in 2021, and shipped in 2022 — I probably haven’t ever anticipated a new game system like I did this one. It’s small and quirky and so fun. I wish I was able to give it even more time, but it’s been such a fun companion for travel or a quick break.

🔗 [Playdate](https://play.date/)

## 📱 iPhone Apps

This is one of the longest section here, which is not surprising because my iPhone is my most-used computer, and these apps help me keep things in my life organized daily.

### PocketCasts

Podcasts are the media I consumed most during 2022 (more than music, tv, and movies combined), and this is the app that helps me listen and organize everything. Yes, it’s better than Spotify and the default Podcasts app on your phone. DM me, I’m happy to share more.

🔗 [App Store](https://itunes.apple.com/au/app/pocket-casts/id414834813)     [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=au.com.shiftyjelly.pocketcasts)

### Apollo

I like Reddit and this app makes it so nice to browse the topics I’m interested in.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/apollo-for-reddit/id979274575)

### Carrot

My favorite weather app of all time shut down in 2022 (RIP DarkSky, 2012 - 2022). Luckily, Carrot has picked up the torch and carried it even further. Nobody needs a weather app with this much functionality. But if you love tracking weather, you might want it.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/id961390574)

### Drafts

Nearly all text I write on my phone or computer starts in Drafts. Notes, grocery lists, emails, restaurant orders, etc. This is my scratch pad. From here, I can easily send the text wherever it belongs. Easily a top-5 app for me.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/id1236254471)

### Things

My preferred to-do list app for non-work task organization.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/id904237743)

### Cellar Tracker

It’s not a pretty app, but it has made keeping track of our wine collection much easier.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/cellartracker/id893759800)     [Web](https://cellartracker.com)

### Pocket

I’ve used a few read-it-later apps over the years, and I finally got back to using Pocket in 2022 to keep up with longer articles I want to… read later.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/id309601447)

### Paprika

The best recipe app. I’m slowly storing family recipes and saving new ones I find online. Great for building grocery lists, too.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/paprika-recipe-manager-3/id1303222868)

### Sleeper

A fun twist on fantasy football apps.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/sleeper-fantasy-sports/id987367543)

### BeReal

A refreshing take on social media. Sometimes buggy, but I enjoyed using this with a few close friends in 2022.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/bereal-your-friends-for-real/id1459645446)

### Letterboxd

I don’t find time to watch nearly as many movies as I would like, so instead I use this to track movies I want to watch, and write reviews of the few I have watched.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/letterboxd/id1054271011)     [Web](https://letterboxd.com)

### Music Smart

I found this in December and I already love it. The app displays details about music albums like you would find in the liner notes in years past. If you’re curious about the people behind the music, you’ll dig it.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/id1512195368)

### Flighty

I look forward to using this anytime I fly. Track all the details in one spot. So good.

🔗 [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/flighty-fast-flight-tracker/id1358823008)

### Ivory

From the makers of Tweetbot, the best Twitter app. Ivory is an app for Mastodon, a social network that’s gaining momentum as internal drama at Twitter plays out.

🔗 [Web](https://tapbots.com/ivory/)

### Gowalla (beta)

This social location app is preparing to relaunch more than a decade after they first debuted. I’ve been loving the beta and can’t wait for it to be public.

🔗 [Web](https://gowalla.com)

## 📚 Books

### Traction

This one has been the driving force behind some new structure we’ve been adding to our teams and processes at work. I’ve enjoyed it so much at work that I’m starting to implement similar systems at home.

🔗 [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Traction-Get-Grip-Your-Business-ebook/dp/B007QWLLV2)

### Good to Great

This was a re-read, it’s been almost a decade since my first time. Jim Collins is very straight-forward so I always enjoy diving back into his writing. This is a great one to shake up your decision making in any area of life.

🔗 [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Good-Great-Some-Companies-Others-ebook/dp/B0058DRUV6)

### 4 Obsessions of a Leader

I love Lencioni and I’m always intrigued to understand more about the minds of successful people, so this one was very enjoyable. Challenging to compare this to the things that fill my own mind day-to-day.

🔗 [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Four-Obsessions-Extraordinary-Executive-Leadership-ebook/dp/B003WUYQOQ)

### The Caledonian Gambit

I started this one just before New Years Eve, but I’m including it because it ended up being my favorite fiction read of the year, and the gateway to the entire [Galactic Cold War](https://dmoren.com/writing/galactic-cold-war/) series from Dan Moren. Highly recommended.

🔗 [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Caledonian-Gambit-Novel-Dan-Moren-ebook/dp/B06XH5T4WG)

## 🎙 Podcasts

This is one of the longest sections in this list, which is no surprise because podcasts topped my media consumption list this year. Believe it or not, this list of favorites represents less than 20% of the podcasts I’m subscribed to currently.

### Decoder with Nilay Patel

Great, in-depth interviews with leadership at major companies, seeking to understand how the leader and their company function internally. Nilay is fantastic, I’m always excited to see who he has next on the show.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/01a33f10-fcfe-0132-18b7-059c869cc4eb)

### Conan O’Brien Needs A Friend

He’s one of the funniest people to me, so to see him interview celebrity friends in a podcast context is always equal parts insightful and hilarious.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/7c388990-aa35-0136-7b93-27f978dac4db)

### How I Built This

Digging into the origin stories of major companies and brands we all know. Fascinating to see how different so many of our favorite names got started.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/a4085f60-5672-0134-ec37-0d50f522381b)

### Reconcilable Differences

Two friends from very different backgrounds trying to understand how each of them ended up the way that they are. Diving into idiosyncrasies, habits, aversions, and rules of life in a very informal way. I laugh a lot, but I always feel like I learn something about how I process the world.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/7e550090-ec2e-0132-1120-059c869cc4eb)

### The End Of The World with Josh Clark

Ten fascinating episodes digging into the future of humanity, potential existential threats, and how to process our place in the universe. I loved this so much and I can’t wait to re-listen in 2023.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/92feaff0-b45a-0136-7b93-27f978dac4db)

### Persona: The French Deception

A true crime investigation into a prolific con artist who tricked people out of millions around the world. Only ten episodes, and a lot of fun.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/786ec390-bab7-013a-d90d-0acc26574db2)

### Stuff You Should Know

If you can’t tell, I love learning, and the two hosts are great at doing a 30-minute to 1-hour deep dive on a wide variety of topics. Informal conversation backed by great research.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/12012c20-0423-012e-f9a0-00163e1b201c)

### Total SF

I love San Francisco, and since moving away this podcast has been a fun way to hear about cultural things going on in the city.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/f87adb40-b38d-0135-9e5f-5bb073f92b78)

### History Daily

A short story about a historical event that happened on this day in history. I love listening to this one in the school drop-off line every morning.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/570f0980-1466-013a-d598-0acc26574db2)

### The Ancients

This year I’ve been doing a deeper dive into ancient history, and this show has been great to supplement some of the other reading I’ve done. Great talks with leading experts.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/6b8378f0-9def-0138-e652-0acc26574db2)

### Upgrade

I listen to a handful of podcasts about technology each week, but in 2022 I started listening to and enjoying this one a lot. The hosts are great, fun, and bring great perspective.

🔗 [Pocket Casts](https://pca.st/podcast/c07ae060-4b18-0132-cd20-5f4c86fd3263)
