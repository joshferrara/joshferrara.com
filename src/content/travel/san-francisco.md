## Food & Drink

### ** ☕ Coffee & Tea**
- **Four Barrel** — Mission District, they have a separate slow bar for pour-overs, and good breakfast pastries.
- **Sightglass** — I think they have 2 locations. This is the most popular SF spot, good stuff, and cool shops.
- **Ritual** — This is one of our favs. Great beans, good branding, and I think they have 2 or 3 locations now.
- **Reveille** — This one is more of a local spot, one of my favorite cappuccinos.
- **Samovar Tea Lounge** — The best chai tea latte ever. Seriously, try it!

### 🥓 **Breakfast**
- **Kitchen Story** — Easily our favorite breakfast in the city. Order the Millionaire Bacon.
- **Plow** — Smaller menu, but also so good. The biscuit and the lemon ricotta pancake are great.
- **Mission Beach Cafe** — If the wait is long at the other two spots, this is a solid 3rd place!
- **Tartine Bakery** — If you want bread things for breakfast, this is the spot. Be prepared to wait in line on the weekend.

### 🥪 **Lunch / Chill Dinner**
- **Umami Burger** — This is always in my top 3 burgers of all time. Seriously, it’s all so good, and the fries are amazing.
- **Tony’s Pizza** — Best pizza. Get the New Yorker and a Meatball Gigante.
- **La Taqueria** — Voted best burrito in the country for a couple years. Also great crunchy tacos!
- **Souvla Greek Cafe** — Greek sandwich and salad spot, rated one of the best restaurants in the city this year! Try the different dips for the fries.
- **Grub** — Awesome sandwich and salad lunch spot, great branding too. Good spot to grab lunch if you're exploring Mission District/shops on Valencia.
- **Boudin Bakery** — Easily one of the most touristy spots in the city, but also some of the best clam chowder you’ll ever eat.
- **Z & Y** — Chinatown eats that are seriously fresh. Try a few things you normally get and then the creamy prawns. You’ll like it.
- **Mission Chinese** — A little more like your average chinese takeout, with a hipster flow. Great for a late night delivery option also.
- **Roam Burger** — This is more like a Hop Doddy or Fat Cow style boutique burger. Very nice.
- San Tung
- Chubby Noodle

### 🍦 **Snacks**
- **Boba Guys** — Bubble tea, milk tea, and other refreshing tea products.
- **Cream** — Ice cream sandwiches on a variety of cookies, open really late.
- **Sidewalk Juice** — Really good acai bows and smoothies!
- **Smitten Ice Cream** — The use liquid nitrogen to make the ice cream as you order it! Fun snack in Hayes Valley.
- **Ghiradelli Square** — Grab a free sample, maybe an ice cream sundae, and enjoy the views of the bay.

### 🥩 **Dinner**
- **Bix** — Jazz lounge and fine dining, hidden away in a tiny alleyway. Fun for a fancy dinner or celebration.
- **Flour & Water** — One of Steve Jobs’ favorite spots, and some of the best pasta I’ve ever had. SO good. It's pricy but worth it.
- **Beretta** — Fancier pizzas and small Italian bites. Busy but fun.

## Activities

### 🛍️ **Shopping**
- **Allsaints** — My favorite store, period. A little pricier, but the clothes last forever. At least stop in just to see the store design.
- **Azalea** — One of our favorite all around shops. Great denim, tshirts, shoes, accessories. Great for guys and girls.
- **Barney's New York** — This is what department stores want to be when they grow up. Best.
- **Nike Store** — It's 7 stories, so you should at least stop by for a quick visit.
- **Apple Store** — This is the new flagship store, where they're testing out a new store open layout with indoor green space. Worth a visit.
- **Le Labo** — They make colognes and candles, and you can even craft your own scent. Y blending theirs. Pretty great stuff.
- **Valencia Street** — This is actually just a street, but it has some of the coolest Mission District shops. Seriously, just walk around. They have a pirate shop, vintage shops, electronic gadgets.

### 🔭 **Sights**
- **Farmers Market** — The Ferry Building is worth seeing anytime, but on Saturday mornings there are TONS of vendors and craftsmen selling their wares. Also a good breakfast spot.
- **Twin Peaks / Sutro Tower** — This is one of the most breathtaking views of the entire city. Watch out for fog, though. If it's rolling in you won't be able to see anything. At all.
- **Battery Park** — Most photos you see of the Golden Gate Bridge with the city in the background were taken from this vantage point. There are multiple stopoff points along the road to pull over and get some photos. If you need bridge pics, this is your spot.
- **Coit Tower** — On one of the many peaks in the center of the city, this is a cool vantage point to see the city from. There is a cost to go inside the tower, but the hill it's on is a great spot to visit on it's own.
- **Sutro Baths** — If you want to see a sunset, this is your spot. Get there 20 minutes early and enjoy an incredible view for the next hour or so.
- **Baker Beach** — This beach runs under the southwest end of the Golden Gate Bridge. Cool views and a chill vibe. The park you drive through to get there is also great.
- **Alcatraz** — A classic tourist spot, but it's definitely worth the visit. Between the line and the ferry ride, you should plan to spend 2-3 hours.

## Notes

### 📱 Helpful apps
- **Uber** — Seriously, this is the city that created Uber, and there's a reason. Rides are typically under 10 bucks and the city is full of drivers so you'll never wait long.
- **GetAround** — Like Airbnb for cars. Rent cars from normal people around the city. Useful for when you decide you need a car for the day. You can usually find a car within a block or two of where you are.
- **Postmates** — Food delivered from just about any restaurant or cafe in town.
- **Detour** — GPS guided audio tours. Cheaper and easier than the typical tourist traps.
- **Routesy** — Makes using all of the different public transit options very easy.
