## Food & Drink

Pine State Biscuits
Best breakfast ever. Order "The Reggie".

Voo Doo Donut
Get the Maple Bacon Bar.

Lardo
Amazing sandwiches.

Portland Food Hall
A bunch of smaller shops with really good stuff. Pizza, ramen, burgers, bowls, ice cream, etc.

Heart Coffee or Barista
Some of the best coffee shops in the country.
- Heart Coffee and Barista are the two incredible coffee shops. Some of the best in the US. Both solid 3rd wave coffee.
- Pine State Biscuits is a breakfast joint I dream about sometimes. Not healthy or clean, but so good.
- Deschutes Brewery is a full on pairing restaurant, so they have all sorts of menu items that match with their huge list of beers. Obviously the food is good even without beer.
- Pok Pok is my favorite Thai place ever, and the chef has gotten so popular he just opened a location in L.A. Get the duck wings.
- Lardo reminds me of Couchon Butcher in New Orleans, and is equally as good. Great for an easy lunch or dinner.

## Activities
- Multnomah Falls is literally right off the interstate, easy little walk and some fun sights. More touristy.
- Wahclella Falls is a little further out I think, but a little better of a hike. Nothing crazy but more off the beaten path and cooler views.
- International Rose Test Garden is a pretty insane sight. Tons of rose varieties they are testing. Cool photos.
- The Grotto is an outdoor Catholic Church built into a cliff and then a giant garden. Pretty cool exploring and photos.
