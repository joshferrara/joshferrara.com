> **Happy New Year!**
> 
> This is year three of cataloging the moments and things that I loved in the previous twelve months. This year, I tried my best to add to my list throughout the year to reduce the end-of-year rush. Although I made some decent progress on that front, it was also still a lot of fun to organize, review, and remember things that I forgot to add throughout the year.
> 
> After three years of putting this list together, I can genuinely say it's personally become one of my favorite ways to look back on the year with gratitude. Not because every year is perfect. In fact, most of them are not. But every year I'm overwhelmed with how many things end up on this list when I'm intentional about remembering and looking back with a thankful eye.
> 
> — [Josh](https://joshferrara.com)

### Shaya in New Orleans

After years of hearing about it, we tried this spot for the first time with Josh &amp; Alyssa and it blew our minds. Definitely some of the best mediterranean food, and honestly some of the best food I’ve had in a long time in any cuisine. Highly recommend. And whatever you do, you’ve gotta get one of the loaded hummus and pita.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.shayarestaurant.com/)

### Bearcat CBD in New Orleans

Not my first visit, but we went again with Josh &amp; Alyssa. It’s one of the top five breakfast spots in my book, and I highly recommend it. There will likely be a wait, and it’s 100% worth your time.

🔗 [**Website**](https://cbd.bearcatcafe.com/)

### Turkey &amp; The Wolf in New Orleans

Alyssa found this spot on Instagram so we checked it out on our New Year trip. Here’s the thing, it’s probably a little too creative for some people, and if that’s you, you probably will know just by looking at the menu. But if this looks good to you? It’ll probably end up being a highlight of your culinary year, too.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.turkeyandthewolf.com/)

### Bar Marilou in New Orleans

This speakeasy-style bar was attached to our hotel, and while there was typically a wait, the hotel allowed us to bypass the line. If you like cocktails that are a little more creative than your standard classics, and you like a vibey spot, this is worth checking out.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.barmarilou.com/eat)

### **Beachbum Berry's Latitude 29**

We were spoiled in San Francisco with a wealth of tiki bars, so any chance I get to visit one these days is a treat. Latitude 29 is a fun one, doesn’t take itself too seriously, but still has plenty of tiki style, and the drinks (and food!) to match.

🔗 [**Website**](https://latitude29nola.com/)

### Blue Oak BBQ in New Orleans

Always a BBQ favorite, this trip was fun because my friend Andrew and I got to meet up with our friend Geoff who was in New Orleans for work. Blue Oak is best when you have enough people to share some appetizers, so this was a win.  We got to watch LSU baseball take home a super regional win against Kentucky, and finished the night with some beignets.

🔗 [**Website**](https://blueoakbbq.com/)

### **El Carlos Elegante in Dallas**

This was a fun meal in Dallas with some of our work team from around the world in town. A Latin restaurant with inspiration from Mexico City and Argentina, and a design style to match. This spot was not what I expected but turned into a new favorite in DFW.

🔗 [**Website**](https://elcarloselegante.com/)

### Perry’s in Grapevine

This one has become a bit of a favorite stop with teammates when in DFW for work. Down the street from our old house in Grapevine, their lobster bisque and ribeye are some of the best around.

🔗 [**Website**](https://perryssteakhouse.com/locations/tx/dallas/grapevine/)

### The Brass Tap in Frisco

Another meetup with Geoff, we were both in DFW for work and found a free night after our meetings to catch up on life. The bar itself was clean and new, impressive local beer list, and it ultimately earned a spot on my list for the good time with a great friend.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.brasstapbeerbar.com/Frisco)

### **Cantinetta Luca in Carmel**

On work trip to California, I ended up a few hours away from Josh &amp; Alyssa, so they drove over to spend the day hanging out. I think Alyssa found this spot on TikTok, and the carbonara &amp; wine list was everything I hoped it would be.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.cantinettaluca.com/)

### The Golden Bee at The Broadmoor

There’s a lot of great food I could talk about at The Broadmoor, but this one is always a favorite. An English pub that was purchased and transported to Colorado, complete with a basement bar and some of the best fish &amp; chips I’ve had in quite a while. This one is now a tradition for me when I’m in Colorado Springs.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.broadmoor.com/dining/golden-bee)

### Joel’s Lobster Rolls

I miss the California coastal seafood options after living back in Louisiana, and one of my favs out there was anywhere with a good lobster roll. Joel’s is a food truck out of New Orleans, and after following on Instagram for a while, I finally got to check them out with Gabi and the boys. So good, I’ll definitely be back the next time I see them around.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.joelslobsterrolls.com/)

### Steaks on the Otto Wilde Grill

For our 13-year anniversary, Gabi got me (us?) an infrared broiler to try our hand at restaurant-style steaks. At 1500º, it can cook a steak in around 4 minutes 🤯 I’ve only tried it out a few times, but the latest was on Christmas night, and was definitely one of the best steaks I’ve made at home. I’m excited about mastering this in 2024.

🔗 [**Website**](https://ottogrills.com/)

### PocketCasts

Podcasts listening came in 2nd place for my most consumed media in 2023, but that accounted for more than 22 days worth of combined listening, and it all happened in this app. I have my subscription management workflow on auto-pilot so I always have a great episode queued up. It’s awesome. If you’re finding yourself getting more invested in regular podcast listening, it’s time to ditch Spotify or the Apple Podcasts app and dive into a dedicated podcast player in 2024.

Last year I said “Nobody *needs* a weather app with this much functionality. But if you love tracking weather, you might *want* it.” I stand by that in 2023. This weather app made me smile on a daily basis, both because of it’s humor, and also because of its advanced features.

Another year of my ongoing love for Drafts. Anything I’m writing beyond a 1 sentence text message starts in Drafts. My scratch pad, synced across all of my devices. ### 1Password

You should start using a password manager in 2024. Store passwords and login details for all of your accounts, keep up with membership numbers, social security numbers, and other secure info. I’ve used this for more than a decade, it’s the best.

🔗 [Web](https://1password.com/)

### Notion

Notion is an extension of my brain. I keep track of so much of my life through this beautiful app. If that sounds like something interesting to you, read this book: [Building a Second Brain](https://www.amazon.com/Building-Second-Brain-Organize-Potential-ebook/dp/B09LVVN9L3?qid=&sr=).

🔗 [Web](https://www.notion.so/)

### Arc Web Browser

I realize switching web browsers is usually only a fun activity for the nerds. And yet, here I am trying to convince you to try out the Arc web browser. It’s built with the same engine as Chrome, so your extensions still work here. But then it adds soooo many quality of life upgrades in every aspect of using the web. It took me a week to get my head around the shift, and since then I’ve fallen deeper in love with it all year long.

🔗 [Web](https://arc.net/)

### Loom

This was originally a work tool, but now I use it in my everyday life. Quickly record a video walkthrough on your computer and share a link that anyone can easily watch. So many apps have tried this, and Loom actually did it in a simple way.

🔗 [Web](https://loom.com/)

### Home Control

If you’re in the Apple Home ecosystem of smart home things, this is a great app for controlling your system from the Mac. I use it to trigger smart devices from my Streamdeck at my desk, and that’s just the tip of the iceberg.

🔗 [Web](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/homecontrol-menu-for-homekit/id1547121417?mt=12)

### Rocket

### Divvy
