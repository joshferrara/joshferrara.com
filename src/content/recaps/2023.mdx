> **Happy New Year!**
> I really enjoyed writing this in [2022](/recaps/2022/), so I’m back again for 2023. I’m not always great at it, but there’s something powerful about reflecting on the year as you kick off a new one. Lessons learned, memories made. 
> 
> My family’s year in 2023 wasn’t all highlights, in fact it was probably our most challenging year yet. We went through, and are still going through, some tough seasons. It’s really easy to look back on the year and only think about those details. 
> 
> But the highlights are worth remembering! In the middle of a hard year, we still have much to thank God for. And I bet you do, too. 
> 
> So, here’s my list of things I loved in 2023, and hopefully it inspires you to spend a few minutes thinking about the things you enjoyed this year, too. 
> 
> — [Josh](https://joshferrara.com)

### New Orleans with Gabi, Josh, &amp; Alyssa

We started the year off by spending a few days in New Orleans with two of our close friends for some relaxing and fun. We stayed at [Maison de la Luz](https://maisondelaluz.com/) (highly recommend!), ate some incredible meals ([Shaya](https://www.shayarestaurant.com/), [Turkey and the Wolf](http://www.turkeyandthewolf.com/), [Bearcat](https://cbd.bearcatcafe.com/), [The Rum House](https://www.therumhouse.com/), [Dickie Brennan’s](https://www.dickiebrennanssteakhouse.com/), [Luke](https://www.lukeneworleans.com/), [Couchon Butcher](https://cochonbutcher.com/)), had some fun drinks &amp; dessert ([Bar Marilou](https://www.barmarilou.com), [Latitude 29](https://latitude29nola.com/), [Drip](https://www.dripaffogato.com/)), enjoyed some music at [Mahogany Jazz Hall](https://www.mjhnola.com/), and relaxed at the [Ritz-Carlton Spa](https://www.ritzcarlton.com/en/hotels/new-orleans/spa). After living near each other for years in San Francisco, it’s always special when we get to spend some quality time with Josh &amp; Alyssa.

### Grocery Mornings with Milo

This year the boys’ school drop-off schedule had us dropping Luca off at 8am and Milo at 9am. This left me with around 45 minutes of free time to hang out with Milo every morning before dropping him off. We experimented with a few different things to do, but his favorite morning activity by far was walking around a grocery store. So 4 days a week, he would make a few laps around the store, say hello to the staff he started to recognize, and get his steps in before his day began. It started as a small way to kill time in our schedule, but it turned into a peaceful start to our day that we both looked forward to.

*Pictures: Grocery walks with Milo, 2023 87 media items kept in the original Notion picture page.*

### Luca’s Kindergarten Graduation

I’ll admit, I’ve never really understood the idea of a kindergarten graduation. Of course, that changed this year when I had a kindergartener who was graduating. To see the transformation that can happen during the course of one school year is incredible and sobering! It goes without saying, but of course I was immensely proud of his progress this year and the kind little guy that he’s growing into. I’ll remember this day for a long time.

### Messenger Cup with Gabi

This year Gabi joined me in Colorado Springs for the [Messenger Cup](https://messengercup.com/) at [The Broadmoor](https://www.broadmoor.com/), a fundraising event put on by one of my clients doing some great work. Although the resort itself is incredible, and the two days of golf on world-class courses doesn’t hurt, the really special part of this trip was getting to introduce Gabi to the team I’ve been working with for four years on this project. Having her there in the room to see the stories from the year and the plans for next year was a very cool moment for me.

### LSU Football with Luca

This is one of those classic moments. I went to LSU football games as a kid with my dad, and I looked forward to being able to do the same with my boys. I took Luca to a game 2 years ago when we first moved back to Louisiana, and although he enjoyed parts of it, he was a little overwhelmed by the day. Fair enough. This year we tried another game and I was so happy about how much he enjoyed it this time! After showing in a few details about the stadium like the scoreboard, the play clock, the band, etc, he was zoned in on everything. By the end of the season he was learning the songs and cheers, and asking what team LSU was playing this week. We ended up going to three games together, and these were some of my favorite days all year.

### LSU Football with Gabi, Luca, &amp; Milo

I’d been trying to decide if Milo was old enough to enjoy an LSU game this year, so when we were given some suite tickets we decided to give it a shot and bring the whole family. If you’re gonna bring a 4-year-old to a football game, I recommend this setup. Milo enjoyed exploring and Luca had fun telling mom and brother all his LSU football knowledge. Milo didn’t last the whole game, but it was such a fun night with all four of us and I’m already looking forward to the next time all four of us can make it to a game together.

### Milo Graduating Feeding Therapy

As a result of Milo’s heart surgery when he was born, he spent the first 7 months of his life being fed with a feeding tube. From there it took him a lot of work to transition to drinking formula from a bottle, and now he’s spent the past two years working with a feeding therapist to transition from formula to solid foods. There were definitely seasons where we wondered if maybe we missed the window. But this summer something started clicking and he was suddenly much more open and adventurous with trying solid foods, and increasing the volume he was eating each week. By the time October came around, his feeding therapist said it was time for him to graduate, he was fully eating on his own! This was a huge milestone for Milo and our family.

*Pictures: Milos feeding therapy progress, October 2021 to October 2023 *October 2021, After losing weight during his hospital stay* 5 media items kept in the original Notion picture page.*

### Dillon Living in Baton Rouge

One aspect of moving around the country like we have is that you sometimes develop close friendships with people you won’t live near again. One of the unexpected lasting friendships we made while living in San Francisco was with our friend, Dillon. While maybe in different phases of life, Dillon was one of those friends that our family just had an quick connection with. He was one of the people that stuck with us during 2019 when our family was going through tough things like Milo’s health challenges and my dad passing away. Dillon’s path has lead him to living all over the country since our SF days, but when work brought him to Baton Rouge for an extended stay, it turned into an unexpected highlight of the year for our family. It included a lot of staying up entirely too late talking, driving to pick up [Pluckers](https://www.pluckers.com/) or [Indian](http://swagatbatonrouge.com/index.html), and I’m grateful we had another year living in the same city again.

*Pictures: Miscellaneous snaps of Dillon throughout 2023 5 media items kept in the original Notion picture page.*

### Playing Golf at Pebble Beach

In October, I had a work trip to Carmel, California for some meetings. My boss and I decided to try our luck and called Pebble Beach to see if we could book a round the next morning. Normally, they're fully booked a year in advance, but we were fortunate to find a last-minute cancellation for 2 people! We changed our flights and got to enjoy a day of golf at Pebble Beach and Spyglass Hill. The courses were challenging and fun, the weather was perfect, and our caddie made the experience even better. A bucket list golf day.

*Pictures: Pebble Beach, November 1, 2023 42 media items kept in the original Notion picture page.*

### Better Than Ezra at L’Auberge

One of the big things my dad passed on to our family was his love of music, and one of the artists I remember him excited about when I was very young was a local band that got some national attention; [*Better Than Ezra*](https://www.betterthanezra.com/). Their biggest hit was arguably in 1993, so you’d be forgiven for not being familiar with their music. They weren’t even my dad’s favorite band, but for some reason their music reminds me of my dad more than many other bands. So of course, when they [played a show in Baton Rouge](https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/better-than-ezra/2023/the-lawn-at-lauberge-casino-and-hotel-baton-rouge-baton-rouge-la-73a1d6d9.html) in November, it ended up being a highlight for me. It was outdoor, raining, and I ended up going solo, but it was such a visceral reminder of so many great moments at live shows with my dad.

<Accordion title="Food &amp; Drink">

### Shaya in New Orleans

After years of hearing about it, we tried this spot for the first time with Josh &amp; Alyssa and it blew our minds. Definitely some of the best mediterranean food, and honestly some of the best food I’ve had in a long time in any cuisine. Highly recommend. And whatever you do, you’ve gotta get one of the loaded hummus and pita.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.shayarestaurant.com/)

### Bearcat CBD in New Orleans

Not my first visit, but we went again with Josh &amp; Alyssa. It’s one of the top five breakfast spots in my book, and I highly recommend it. There will likely be a wait, and it’s 100% worth your time.

🔗 [**Website**](https://cbd.bearcatcafe.com/)

### Turkey &amp; The Wolf in New Orleans

Alyssa found this spot on Instagram so we checked it out on our New Year trip. Here’s the thing, it’s probably a little too creative for some people, and if that’s you, you probably will know just by looking at the menu. But if this looks good to you? It’ll probably end up being a highlight of your culinary year, too.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.turkeyandthewolf.com/)

### Bar Marilou in New Orleans

This speakeasy-style bar was attached to our hotel, and while there was typically a wait, the hotel allowed us to bypass the line. If you like cocktails that are a little more creative than your standard classics, and you like a vibey spot, this is worth checking out.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.barmarilou.com/eat)

### **Beachbum Berry's Latitude 29**

We were spoiled in San Francisco with a wealth of tiki bars, so any chance I get to visit one these days is a treat. Latitude 29 is a fun one, doesn’t take itself too seriously, but still has plenty of tiki style, and the drinks (and food!) to match.

🔗 [**Website**](https://latitude29nola.com/)

### Blue Oak BBQ in New Orleans

Always a BBQ favorite, this trip was fun because my friend Andrew and I got to meet up with our friend Geoff who was in New Orleans for work. Blue Oak is best when you have enough people to share some appetizers, so this was a win.  We got to watch LSU baseball take home a super regional win against Kentucky, and finished the night with some beignets.

🔗 [**Website**](https://blueoakbbq.com/)

### **El Carlos Elegante in Dallas**

This was a fun meal in Dallas with some of our work team from around the world in town. A Latin restaurant with inspiration from Mexico City and Argentina, and a design style to match. This spot was not what I expected but turned into a new favorite in DFW.

🔗 [**Website**](https://elcarloselegante.com/)

### Perry’s in Grapevine

This one has become a bit of a favorite stop with teammates when in DFW for work. Down the street from our old house in Grapevine, their lobster bisque and ribeye are some of the best around.

🔗 [**Website**](https://perryssteakhouse.com/locations/tx/dallas/grapevine/)

### The Brass Tap in Frisco

Another meetup with Geoff, we were both in DFW for work and found a free night after our meetings to catch up on life. The bar itself was clean and new, impressive local beer list, and it ultimately earned a spot on my list for the good time with a great friend.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.brasstapbeerbar.com/Frisco)

### **Cantinetta Luca in Carmel**

On work trip to California, I ended up a few hours away from Josh &amp; Alyssa, so they drove over to spend the day hanging out. I think Alyssa found this spot on TikTok, and the carbonara &amp; wine list was everything I hoped it would be.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.cantinettaluca.com/)

### The Golden Bee at The Broadmoor

There’s a lot of great food I could talk about at The Broadmoor, but this one is always a favorite. An English pub that was purchased and transported to Colorado, complete with a basement bar and some of the best fish &amp; chips I’ve had in quite a while. This one is now a tradition for me when I’m in Colorado Springs.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.broadmoor.com/dining/golden-bee)

### Joel’s Lobster Rolls

I miss the California coastal seafood options after living back in Louisiana, and one of my favs out there was anywhere with a good lobster roll. Joel’s is a food truck out of New Orleans, and after following on Instagram for a while, I finally got to check them out with Gabi and the boys. So good, I’ll definitely be back the next time I see them around.

🔗 [**Website**](https://www.joelslobsterrolls.com/)

### Steaks on the Otto Wilde Grill

For our 13-year anniversary, Gabi got me (us?) an infrared broiler to try our hand at restaurant-style steaks. At 1500º, it can cook a steak in around 4 minutes 🤯 I’ve only tried it out a few times, but the latest was on Christmas night, and was definitely one of the best steaks I’ve made at home. I’m excited about mastering this in 2024.

🔗 [**Website**](https://ottogrills.com/)

<Accordion title="Books">

> At the start of 2023 I wanted to try to replace some of my entertainment time (mainly social media and TV) with reading. I’ve loved reading my whole life, but it was one of the things that fell in priority as adult life filled my time.
> 
> So I tried to make a conscious effort to fill in gaps in my day with a few pages here and there. When I was in the car or doing chores around the house, I’d switch to the audio version of a book. And little by little, I rebuilt my reading habit again. 
> 
> I set a goal to read 20 books in 2023, and I’m ending the year with [54 books read](https://www.goodreads.com/review/list/53614472-josh-ferrara?shelf=2023). 🤯
> 
> You can see [the full list on my Goodreads profile](https://www.goodreads.com/review/list/53614472-josh-ferrara?shelf=2023), but the book listed here were my favorites from the year.

### Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat

This put words to so many food concepts I’ve noticed but never understood. If you like cooking or just exploring great food, you should read this.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/6076583908)

### Project Hail Mary

One of the best sci-fi story lines in recent memory. Unique and so good. Fingers crossed for a movie soon.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5516914115)

### The Martian

The movie is great, the book was even better. If you liked the movie this is one that has some great additional story.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5917227071)

### Elon Musk

One of the best biographies I’ve ever read. Like or hate Elon, his life and this book are fascinating.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5988183495)

### American Prometheus

I missed Oppenheimer in theaters, so I decided to read the book while I waited for the digital release. So glad I did. A truly fascinating human.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5731208098)

### The Redemptive Business

The company I work for employs these concepts in our culture and this book is a fantastic intro to the ideas.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5664891466)

### The Obstacle Is The Way

This book was probably the most important one I read this year. In a season of challenges, this offered a much-needed perspective shift for me. It was also my entry into other stoic writings. I highly recommend this one for everyone.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5569488551)

### Chip War

This one is deep and thorough, and easily the best source I’ve ever found if you’re curious to understand how computer chip production is affected by global dynamics. I loved this one.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5443254471)

### Chokepoint Capitalism

This one may upset you to find how many industries are rigging regulations in a way to benefit themselves at the detriment of consumers. But it’s super helpful to be informed about, and will help you be a wise buyer. Also Cory Doctorow was a new favorite author this year.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5393097426)

### The Nova Incident

The latest in a sci-fi series by one of my favorite authors, this was the best so far. I highly recommend checking out “The Galactic Cold War” series.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5389143873)

### Red Team Blues

My first fiction read from Cory Doctorow, and I really enjoyed it. Tech, spies, silicon valley.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5526820946)

### In The Garden of Beasts

My first Erik Larson book, and an incredible dive into his style of writing, told through direct quotes from journals, diaries, official records, letters to friends, and memoirs. Hitler’s Germany from the perspective of an American ambassador. Very good.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5556567795)

### It’s All Too Much

I read this years ago and decided to revisit it this year. A fantastic and quick read about our emotional relationship with possessions and how it influences our ability to let go and declutter our lives.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5565939145)

### Everydata

My sister suggested I’d like this book and she was spot on. I’ve found myself skeptical of certain surveys or data in the past, but this puts insight into why not all data is equal.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5612965696)

### 1177 B.C.: The Year Civilization Collapsed

One of my fascinations this year was the unexplained collapse of the Bronze Age. This was my favorite book on the subject so far. Extremely thorough. Fair warning: There is *a* *lot* of historical context it has to walk you through before getting to the ultimate theory the book is presenting. But it’s ultimately the most big-picture understanding I’ve seen so far.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5661702418)

### Extremely Online

I’ve followed Taylor Lorenz’s tech journalism for more than a decade, so I was excited when she released this book about the history of internet culture. It’s fun, fascinating, and wild seeing how moments of the early internet have affected our influencer-driven world today.

🔗 [My Review](https://www.goodreads.com/review/show/5563293643)
