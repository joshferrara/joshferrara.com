During my first 30 years of life I spent 5 nights in the hospital, including my own birth and the birth of my first son. In the past 2 years, I’ve spent a little over 30 nights in hospitals with my dad & my second son.

## A Few Thoughts

1. **Hospital staff (especially pediatrics) are heroes. Thank them.** From basic hospitality to care through the night, these people give their lives to make some our most tense moments a little more comfortable.

2. **Asking questions when your medical team is in the room is a great way to ease your stress.** Uncertainty adds to anxiety. Write down your questions so you remember to ask when the team visits.

3. **Waiting is the name of game. Pack accordingly.** An iPad, book, or game to play with can go a long way to fill the time between vitals checks and bedtime. Don’t forget chargers.

4. **Eating is important. Even if you’re not hungry.** It’s easy to skip meals when you’re anxious, but your body still needs fuel whether you feel like it or not. And no, coffee doesn’t count.

5. **Basic comforts aren’t selfish.** Grab your pillow from home, a hoodie, or some comfy socks. Even if you’re not the patient, a few little amenities can make your time a lot nicer. (My starter packing list is below!)

6. **If all else fails, writing Twitter threads is a great use of your time. 🙂**
