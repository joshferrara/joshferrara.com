### A Field Guide to Planning for Large Groups

Congrats! You’ve made some great friends. Unfortunately, your group has grown and it’s now becoming slightly complicated to make plans for the whole crew. Fear not; the tips in this handy little guide can help you make light work out of wrangling even the largest #squad.

One important note to remember: this is one of the few social situations in life where there is no clearly designated leader. Business and family come packaged with a clear team captain. Friendship is the wild wild west. You’re dealing with peers! Which is why this can be both fun and difficult at the same time.

The number one biggest factor in a successful group outing is having and following a decent plan. It doesn’t have to come from the same person every time, and it doesn’t have to be rigid, but when it’s your turn to plan, step up, and when it’s not you, stand down, soldier.

This may all seem like common sense, and for the most part, it is. The tricky part is that when we are most comfortable with our friends, we also feel most comfortable expressing our raw opinions, which is typically a gateway drug to unadulterated selfishness. Being a team player doesn’t always come naturally, but here are a few tips to make sure you’re never part of the problem.

### Organizers

- **Handle problems.** — You organized this party, and this crowd expects you to play a leading role when problems arise. Set the tone by helping keep the night moving along smoothly.

- **Clearly communicate plans.** — Share the date, time, and location clearly and directly. Other details of the plan can be fuzzy, but lack of clarity around these 3 is a sure way to have a stressful outing.

- **Collect RSVPs.** — Ask who’s coming, follow up with a reminder, and get an accurate count for your group. Knowing who is involved helps with seating, transportation, and it helps you handle problems.

- **Make a reservation.** — This is the single greatest preparation you can make for your crew to ensure a solid outing. You may have to call around, but reservations for a large party make all the difference.

- **Be considerate.** — A little goes a long way. Plan for friends joining with kids. Be flexible if people are coming after work. Choose a restaurant that will be a crowd pleaser. Think ahead for the crew.

### Attendees

- **RSVP.** — Whether you’re joining or not, a clear “yes” or “no” makes planning simpler. Be sure to include kids in your count.

- **Avoid last-minute invites.** — Adding a friend to the list at the last minute can throw a wrench in even the best plans. If you’re going to bring an extra guest, it’s your responsibility to help adjust the plans or reservations to accommodate the new total.

- **Carry some cash.** — Valet. Cash-only taco joints. It may be the 21st century, but some of the best parts of your night may require cash. Don’t be the friend that needs a detour to find an ATM. Be prepared.

- **Don’t try to change plans.** — Nothing can throw a wrench in plans quicker than multiple chiefs trying to change the plans. If you didn’t organize the group plans, be respectful and don’t try to change them.

- **Be considerate.** — Remember that you are part of a group here. Be a team player. Don’t be shy about what you want to do, but remember that a little bit of selfishness can spoil a group outing quickly.

---

The goal here is being the kind of person you would be friends with. Nobody is perfect, but with a little consideration, your group’s size doesn’t have to keep everyone from having a great time together.
